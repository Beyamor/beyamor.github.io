One flashy but very purposeless feature is a bit of image sorcery. Now, large images are resized on the server side and every one gets this neat little window thing that shows off its full glory when you click on it. Here, check it out.

Mind you, there's no maximum size constraint right now, so a big picture will spill right off the left side of the page. However, it's a leap in the right direction. I can't say I have much in mind for the pictures, but doing that did let me play around with some regular expressions, which is always rad.

Regular expressions describe patterns of text. That is, instead of checking whether something matches a bit of text exactly, you can check whether something matches the regular expression. For example, 'ya.', which uses the wildcard character '.', matches 'yam' and 'yay' and anything else that starts with 'ya' and ends with another character.

One of the processors I'm using just checks through the file for any image tags. A regular expression pulls out the image source and, if it needs to be scaled down, the image's height and width are adjusted accordingly. Just that simple. Maybe not terribly efficient, but hey, who needs to rush.

The other thing is a little one, but nice. I've justed added a "publish now" button for the blog editor. This does a couple of things. First, and more boring, the blog is automatically set to publish and save, which is pretty much what you'd expect. The second, and a little cooler, is that, using a Twitter intent, a tweet based on the blog is created. Then, if I'm feeling like spamming the poor fools who follow me, it's as simple as the push of a button. I was kind of aiming to make the tweets entirely automatic, but after a couple minutes of research, it didn't seem worth the hassle.
